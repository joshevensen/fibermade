# Stage 2 — Fibermade as the Selling Surface

## Purpose
Stage 2 exists to make Fibermade the **primary selling surface** for hand-dyed yarn businesses.

This is the stage where Fibermade moves from:
- operational spine  
to  
- operational spine **plus** customer-facing sales

Stage 2 introduces:
- a Fibermade-hosted website
- checkout and order capture
- discounts and promotions
- show and event modeling

All while preserving Fibermade’s production-first worldview.

---

## Core Shift from Stage 1
Before Stage 2:
- Shopify owns all buyer-facing interactions
- Fibermade owns operations only

After Stage 2:
- Fibermade owns both **operations and sales intent**
- External tools are optional or secondary

Production reality remains the center of truth.

---

## Core Capabilities (Stage 2)

### Website & Catalog
- Fibermade-hosted storefront
- Product presentation derived from:
  - colorways
  - bases
  - availability (not raw inventory truth)
- Opinionated structure optimized for yarn sales, not generic ecommerce

The website is a projection of production reality, not an independent system.

---

### Checkout & Orders
- Fibermade-owned checkout
- Orders immediately integrate with:
  - inventory reservation
  - production planning
  - Dye Lists
- Shopify is no longer required

Checkout is designed to serve production clarity first, conversion second.

---

### Discounts & Promotions
- Discounts move fully into Fibermade
- Discount intent is defined centrally
- Supported discount types remain opinionated and limited
- No arbitrary rule builders

Discounts are treated as operational decisions, not marketing hacks.

---

### Shows & Events
Shows become a first-class concept in Stage 2.

- Events can be defined in Fibermade
- Inventory can be allocated to a show
- Sales can be recorded post-event
- Show performance informs future planning

Real-time POS is not required at this stage.

---

## Non-Goals (Stage 2)
Stage 2 does **not** attempt to:
- Be POS-first
- Support unlimited customization of storefronts
- Solve accounting or taxes
- Compete with enterprise ecommerce platforms
- Handle complex multi-location logistics

---

## Exit Criteria (Stage 2 → Stage 3)
Stage 2 is successful when:
- Fibermade-hosted sites are used in production
- Orders are placed directly through Fibermade
- Discounts and events are managed in Fibermade
- Shows are planned and reconciled without external tools
- Users no longer depend on Shopify for core workflows

Only once Fibermade reliably owns checkout intent should card-present payments expand.
